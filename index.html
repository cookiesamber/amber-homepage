THRIFTING

Well clothes,shoes,room decor,and books are getting more and more pricey so where can you find cheap stuff if your a broke teen like me, I have a solution. It's called the thrift store they have everything from tops to decor to shoes and it's super cheap. I would much rather shop at thrift stores then fast fashion stores like forever 21 because everyone wears that stuff and the clothes get thrown away after no one is buying the clothing item anymore or it's not the season. Thrift stores help the environment by having someone buy it and then donate it to a thrift store where someone can wear or use the item. Also you can wear clothes from different years and you get to own one-of-a-kind peices which can help you experiment with styles and help you become more confident in wearing something different than what others are wearing. So you should thrift because you can help recylcle and reuse clothing also you can create and experiment with your style so you should try it out because it's kind of like going on a treasure hunt finding the right peice of clothing. If you do decide to try thrifting here a couple of tips to help you on your "treasure hunt". Tip #1 is make sure you look through everything because you might over look a section and it might have exactly what your looking for, so make sure to look through every all the sections. Tip #2 is have an open mind, for me I usaully go in with a general idea lets say I want to look for a jacket it may end up being a sweat shirt or a wind breaker you don't want to have a specific idea because if you don't end up finding that item it can be upsetting for you. Tip #3 is listen to music, listening to music can get you into the mood of shopping and more energized I actually got this tip from the youtuber "the sweetest peach" she does a lot of thrift hauls and their always very good and informative. Tip #4 is find out when the store you thrift as has sales going on this can help you save even more money different thrift stores have different sales some have sales like 50% off on certaint colored tags or stuff a bag for $5 so find out what sales your local thrift store has. Tip #5 is go thrifting often maybe twice every week or once every week . You could also choose to only go thrifting on sales days. Tip #6 is try on everything. I specifically always try on jeans because I have a small waist and different jeans have different cuts or ways they fit on a body, I dont't try on tops unless their cut a different way or their oversized. Jackets manage to fit me well, I also try on shorts because their cut a different way and I don't typically but a lot of skirts from the thrift store also if the item does'nt fir but you already bought it that's ok because you can just donate it to another thrift store and they usually give you credits for donating clothes. Tip #7 is make freinds with the workers and other frequent shoppers this can make the whole shopping experience even more enjoyable.
